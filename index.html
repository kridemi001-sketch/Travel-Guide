Some decisions that save time

Pack light → Carrying less means faster movement through stations, airports, and cabs. Book trains/buses online in advance → Use IRCTC, RedBus, or MakeMyTrip to avoid last-minute ticket lines. Choose morning flights/trains → Less likely to be delayed and helps you reach early to explore more. Book local transport online → Use Ola, Uber, or Rapido instead of bargaining for rickshaws. Eat where locals eat → Saves time (and money) by avoiding tourist traps and long waits. Plan visits by area → Group nearby attractions on the same day to reduce travel time. Avoid peak hours in cities → Big cities like Delhi, Mumbai, or Bangalore have heavy rush between 8–10 AM and 5–8 PM.

Packing list (essentials, not excess)

  • Valid I'd
  • Quick-dry clothes, one smart outfit for evenings
  • Small first-aid and blister kit
  • Portable battery and a compact travel towel
  • Copies of important documents and a small stash of local cash
  • Small and Comfortable backpack

Where to eat and how to order

Seek food market stalls and family-run tavernas for the most authentic meal at a fraction of tourist prices. Ask for the "catch of the day" or the house special — in many places these are the most affordable and freshly prepared options.
